# BUSINESS ASSOCIATE AGREEMENT

**Between:** [Customer Legal Name] ("Covered Entity")  
**And:** Weybre AI ("Business Associate")  
**Effective Date:** [Date]

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Covered Entity is a Covered Entity under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA");

WHEREAS, Business Associate provides services to Covered Entity that may involve the use or disclosure of Protected Health Information ("PHI");

WHEREAS, the parties wish to comply with HIPAA and the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act ("HITECH Act");

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements herein, the parties agree as follows:

## 1. DEFINITIONS

Terms used but not otherwise defined in this Agreement shall have the meanings set forth in HIPAA and the HITECH Act.

1.1 **"Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** means information that relates to the past, present, or future physical or mental health or condition of an individual, the provision of health care to an individual, or payment for health care, and that identifies the individual or could reasonably be used to identify the individual.

1.2 **"Services"** means the Weybre AI platform and related services provided to Covered Entity.

## 2. PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES

2.1 Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only as permitted by this Agreement or as required by law.

2.2 Business Associate may use PHI to perform Services for Covered Entity as specified in the underlying Services Agreement.

2.3 Business Associate may use PHI for proper management and administration of Business Associate or to carry out legal responsibilities.

2.4 Business Associate may disclose PHI as required by law.

2.5 Business Associate shall not use or disclose PHI in any manner that would violate HIPAA if done by Covered Entity.

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

3.1 **Safeguards:** Business Associate shall implement appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of PHI other than as permitted by this Agreement.

3.2 **Minimum Necessary:** Business Associate shall use, disclose, and request only the minimum necessary PHI to accomplish the intended purpose.

3.3 **Subcontractors:** Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of Business Associate agree to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to Business Associate.

3.4 **Reporting:** Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any use or disclosure of PHI not provided for by this Agreement, including breaches of unsecured PHI, within 10 business days of discovery.

3.5 **Individual Rights:** Business Associate shall:
- Provide access to PHI in a Designated Record Set to Covered Entity or an individual within 10 business days of request
- Make PHI available for amendment and incorporate amendments as directed by Covered Entity
- Document disclosures and make information available for accounting of disclosures

3.6 **Compliance:** Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of HHS for purposes of determining compliance with HIPAA.

## 4. SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

4.1 Business Associate shall implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of electronic PHI.

4.2 Specific security measures include:
- **Encryption:** AES-256 encryption at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit
- **Access Controls:** Role-based access control, multi-factor authentication
- **Audit Controls:** Comprehensive logging of PHI access and modifications
- **Transmission Security:** Secure protocols for all PHI transmission
- **Integrity Controls:** Mechanisms to ensure PHI is not improperly altered or destroyed

4.3 Business Associate shall conduct regular risk assessments and implement security measures to reduce risks to a reasonable and appropriate level.

## 5. BREACH NOTIFICATION

5.1 Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity of any breach of unsecured PHI without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 10 business days after discovery.

5.2 The notification shall include:
- Identification of each individual whose PHI has been breached
- Description of the breach, including date and circumstances
- Types of PHI involved
- Steps individuals should take to protect themselves
- Steps Business Associate is taking to investigate, mitigate, and prevent recurrence
- Contact information for individuals to ask questions

5.3 Business Associate shall cooperate with Covered Entity in meeting Covered Entity's breach notification obligations under HIPAA.

## 6. OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

6.1 Covered Entity shall provide Business Associate with the Notice of Privacy Practices that Covered Entity produces in accordance with HIPAA.

6.2 Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any limitation in its Notice of Privacy Practices that affects Business Associate's use or disclosure of PHI.

6.3 Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any changes in, or revocation of, permission by an individual to use or disclose PHI.

6.4 Covered Entity shall not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would not be permissible under HIPAA if done by Covered Entity.

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

7.1 This Agreement shall be effective as of the Effective Date and shall terminate when all PHI provided by Covered Entity to Business Associate is destroyed or returned to Covered Entity.

7.2 Upon termination of the Services Agreement, Business Associate shall:
- Return or destroy all PHI received from Covered Entity that Business Associate maintains in any form
- Retain no copies of the PHI
- Provide written certification of destruction or return

7.3 If return or destruction is not feasible, Business Associate shall:
- Extend the protections of this Agreement to such PHI
- Limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible
- Provide written explanation of why return or destruction is not feasible

7.4 Covered Entity may terminate this Agreement immediately if Business Associate breaches a material term and does not cure the breach within 30 days of notice.

## 8. INDEMNIFICATION

8.1 Business Associate shall indemnify and hold harmless Covered Entity from any claims, damages, or penalties arising from Business Associate's breach of this Agreement or violation of HIPAA.

## 9. MISCELLANEOUS

9.1 **Regulatory Changes:** The parties agree to take such action as is necessary to amend this Agreement to comply with changes in HIPAA or other applicable laws.

9.2 **Interpretation:** Any ambiguity in this Agreement shall be resolved to permit Covered Entity to comply with HIPAA.

9.3 **Survival:** The obligations of Business Associate under this Agreement shall survive termination of the Services Agreement.

9.4 **Governing Law:** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws specified in the Services Agreement.
